One of the largest projects I worked on involved analyzing NFL injury data to identify factors associated with lower-body injuries and concussions. As part of a team capstone project, I focused on data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and data visualization to help uncover patterns in player injuries and support downstream machine learning models.

**GitHub Repository:** [final-capstone](https://github.com/agaffney-hub/final-capstone)

## Project Overview

The project combined multiple NFL injury datasets from Kaggle, including lower-body injury records and concussion data. The goal was to determine which factors—including field conditions, player position, weather, game conditions, and player movement—were most strongly associated with injuries sustained during NFL games.

## My Contributions

### Data Cleaning

A significant portion of the project involved preparing messy real-world datasets for analysis. I helped standardize and transform data from multiple sources by:

- Consolidating dozens of stadium and weather categories into usable classifications.
- Standardizing player positions and play types.
- Cleaning missing and invalid temperature values.
- Converting categorical variables into formats suitable for analysis and visualization.
- Merging injury, tracking, and game-level datasets into analysis-ready tables.
- Creating reusable Python functions to automate data preparation workflows.

### Exploratory Data Analysis

Using Python and SQL, I explored relationships between injuries and game conditions. Key findings included:

- Linebackers experienced the highest rates of both concussions and lower-body injuries.
- Concussions during punt plays occurred far more frequently than lower-body injuries.
- Injury severity showed a relationship with playing surface type.
- Certain field locations and player positions exhibited higher injury concentrations.

### Data Visualization

I developed visualizations using Python and Tableau to communicate injury patterns and support stakeholder understanding.

Visualizations highlighted:

- Injury locations across the football field.
- Injury frequency by player position.
- Relationships between temperature and injury occurrence.
- Differences in injury duration between natural grass and synthetic turf.
- Interactive dashboards that allowed users to explore injury trends by position, injury type, and field conditions.
